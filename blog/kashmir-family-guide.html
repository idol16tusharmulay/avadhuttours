7 Days in Kashmir with Family — A Complete Travel Guide

✍ Avadhut Tours Team 📅 June 2026 📖 5 min read 📍 Kashmir

There's a reason Kashmir is called Paradise on Earth — and it's not just a tourism slogan. When you're gliding across Dal Lake at dawn with snow-dusted peaks reflected in the water, or watching your kids see real snow for the very first time at Gulmarg, you understand why people return to Kashmir year after year. We've been organising family trips to Kashmir for over a decade, and every single time it delivers.

This guide covers how to spend 7 days in Kashmir as a family — including what's genuinely good for kids, which spots work well for senior family members, and the practical stuff nobody tells you until it's too late.

Why Kashmir Works So Well for Families

Kashmir has something rare: it's visually dramatic without being physically punishing. Unlike Leh-Ladakh (which demands acclimatisation and is tough for heart patients or seniors), Kashmir's main destinations sit between 1,600 and 2,800 metres altitude. Most families — including those travelling with grandparents or young children — can handle it comfortably.

The tourism infrastructure is mature. Hotels ranging from budget guesthouses to heritage houseboats are plentiful in Srinagar and Pahalgam. Shikara rides, gondola trips, horse riding — there are activities for every age group. And Kashmiri food, especially the Wazwan cuisine, is deeply satisfying comfort food that even picky eaters tend to enjoy.

Best Time to Visit Kashmir with Family

April to October is the sweet spot for family trips. Here's the quick breakdown:

  • April–May: Chinar trees in bloom, tulip gardens in full flower (the Srinagar Tulip Garden is Asia's largest), pleasant temperatures of 10–22°C. Ideal for families who want greenery and colour.
  • June–August: Peak summer, Gulmarg's gondola is fully operational, Pahalgam meadows are lush green. Temperatures reach 25–28°C in Srinagar but Gulmarg and Pahalgam remain cool. School holiday season — book early.
  • September–October: Our personal recommendation for families. The tourist rush eases, the Chinar trees turn gold and amber, and the skies are crystal clear. Prices drop slightly and hotels are easier to book.

Avoid November to March for first-time family visits — mountain passes can close, some hotels shut for the season, and altitude cold is serious (Gulmarg drops to -14°C in January).

A 7-Day Kashmir Family Itinerary

Day 1
Arrive Srinagar — Dal Lake & Shikara Ride

Fly into Sheikh ul-Alam International Airport and transfer directly to your houseboat or hotel on Dal Lake. Freshen up, then take an evening Shikara ride — 1 to 2 hours on the lake with the Zabarwan mountains turning orange in the sunset. Stop at the floating vegetable market if timing allows. Light Kashmiri dinner on board.

Day 2
Gulmarg — Gondola & Snow Play

Early start (leave by 7:30 AM) for the 53 km drive to Gulmarg. The Gulmarg Gondola is Asia's highest cable car and the kids will remember this for years. Phase 1 takes you to Kongdori (2,600m) where there's snow almost year-round. Phase 2 goes higher to Apharwat Peak (3,980m) — only for fit adults without heart conditions. Return by evening, dinner in Srinagar.

Day 3
Pahalgam — Betaab Valley & Aru Valley

Drive to Pahalgam (96 km, about 2.5 hours). Check into your hotel, then explore Betaab Valley — made famous by the 1983 Bollywood film, it's a gorgeous river valley with very easy walking paths suitable for everyone. Alternatively, hire horses to Baisaran Meadow (called the 'Mini Switzerland'). Pahalgam town has good cafés and local handicraft shops.

Day 4
Pahalgam — Chandanwari & Lidder River

Stay a second night in Pahalgam and explore further. Chandanwari (28 km from Pahalgam) has snow almost through summer and is the starting point of the Amarnath Yatra. River-side walks along the Lidder River are perfect for families. Trout fishing is available if your group is interested — kids genuinely love it.

Day 5
Back to Srinagar — Old City & Local Markets

Return to Srinagar and spend the afternoon in the old city. Jama Masjid, Shah Hamdan Shrine, and the narrow lanes of old Srinagar give you a real sense of Kashmiri culture. The Lal Chowk market area is great for Pashmina shopping — and yes, real Pashmina shawls do exist here, though you need to know how to identify them (we'll brief you before). Wazwan dinner at a local restaurant tonight.

Day 6
Mughal Gardens — Nishat, Shalimar & Chashme Shahi

A leisurely half-day in the famous Mughal Gardens along the shores of Dal Lake. Nishat Bagh (Garden of Gladness) has 12 terraced levels rising up the hillside — stunning for photos. Shalimar Bagh, built by Emperor Jahangir, is the grandest of the three. Chashme Shahi is smaller but has the famous natural spring. Afternoon: another Shikara ride or visit to the Hazratbal Shrine. Final dinner on the houseboat.

Day 7
Departure Day

Morning at leisure — last-minute shopping for saffron, Kashmiri dry fruits, and Kahwa tea (all excellent gifts). Transfer to Srinagar Airport for your flight home. Most flights depart in the morning, so plan accordingly.

Practical Tips for Families

  • Pack warm clothes, even in summer. Gulmarg at Kongdori level has snow year-round. A fleece and windproof jacket are essential, even in June. Gulmarg rental jackets exist but quality is inconsistent.
  • Book the Gondola in advance. During peak season (May–July), Phase 2 tickets sell out by 9 AM. Your tour operator should pre-book these. If doing it yourself, reach Gulmarg by 7:30 AM and go straight to the ticket counter.
  • The houseboat experience is genuinely special. Don't skip it thinking it's a tourist gimmick. A night or two on Dal Lake — waking up to mist over the water, having breakfast served by the lake owners — is something families remember for decades. Choose Category A or Deluxe houseboats for better hygiene and comfort.
  • Photography at Dal Lake is best at dawn. The light at 6–7 AM is extraordinary. If you're a photography enthusiast, wake up early — you won't regret it.
  • Kashmiri food to try: Rogan Josh (slow-cooked lamb), Yakhni (yoghurt-based lamb curry), Haakh saag (Kashmiri greens), Sheermal (saffron-flavoured bread), and Phirni for dessert. For kids who won't eat spicy food, most restaurants happily do plain rice and dal.

What Kids Absolutely Love

The Gondola at Gulmarg is the undisputed highlight for children — snow at 3,000 metres is simply magical. Shikara rides on Dal Lake (especially if you get a colourful decorated boat) are endlessly fun. Horse riding to Baisaran Meadow in Pahalgam is another favourite. Floating markets on the lake — where vendors paddle up to sell bread, flowers, and trinkets from their shikaras — fascinate kids of all ages.

Senior-Friendly Aspects

Kashmir is one of the more manageable Himalayan destinations for senior travellers. The main sightseeing areas are accessible without significant trekking. Gulmarg's Phase 1 Gondola is fine for most seniors — Phase 2 should be skipped by those with cardiac conditions or breathing issues. Houseboats have steps, so those with mobility problems should opt for a hotel instead. Mughal Gardens have paved paths throughout. We always recommend a dedicated tour manager for groups with seniors — they handle permits, queue management, and make sure the pace isn't rushed.
