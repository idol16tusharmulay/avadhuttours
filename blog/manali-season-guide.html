Manali in Summer vs Winter — When Is the Best Time to Go?

✍ Avadhut Tours Team 📅 June 2026 📖 5 min read 📍 Manali, Himachal Pradesh

Manali is one of those destinations that's genuinely good at different times of year — but for completely different reasons. People who visit in summer and people who visit in winter often sound like they went to two different places. Both are right. The real question isn't "which season is better?" but "which season is better for what I want?"

We've been running Manali tours from Navi Mumbai for over 15 years, and we get this question every single week. Here's the honest, complete answer.

Summer in Manali (April to June)

☀️ Summer Season 8°C – 28°C

This is Manali at its most alive. The Beas River is roaring from snowmelt, the valleys are shockingly green, wildflowers are everywhere, and Rohtang Pass is usually open from mid-May onwards. Summer is the season for adventure activities — and there are plenty.

  • Rohtang Pass open (mid-May to mid-October) — permits required, book 2–3 days ahead
  • River rafting on the Beas (Grade 2–4, suitable from beginners to experienced)
  • Paragliding at Solang Valley and Dobhi
  • Mountain biking, trekking to Hampta Pass, Kheerganga
  • Lush Solang Valley for horse riding and zorbing
  • Drive to Spiti Valley possible if roads are clear
Best for: Adventure seekers, families with active kids, first-time visitors

The flip side of summer is crowds. May and June are peak season — hotels fill up fast, Rohtang Pass queues can be 3–4 hours on weekends, and prices are at their highest. Booking 45–60 days in advance is essential for good hotel options in summer.

Winter in Manali (December to February)

❄️ Winter Season -10°C – 12°C

Manali in winter is a completely different animal. The town itself receives snowfall from December, and Solang Valley becomes a proper ski slope. Rohtang Pass is closed, so you won't get that crossing — but you don't need it. There's enough snow right here. Hotels cut rates significantly, crowds thin out, and the whole experience feels quieter and more intimate.

  • Skiing and snowboarding at Solang Valley (equipment rental available on-site)
  • Snow activities: sledging, snow tubing, snowball fights — kids love it
  • Atal Tunnel (opened 2020) now keeps Lahaul connected year-round — opens up Sissu, Gramphu
  • Cosy wood-fire restaurants, Himachali thukpa and momos
  • 25–30% cheaper hotels with easier availability
  • Crisp clear skies and dramatic mountain photography
Best for: Honeymoon couples, snow lovers, budget travellers, photography enthusiasts

What to watch out for in winter: road conditions can be tricky after heavy snowfall. Always travel with an experienced driver who knows these roads. Night driving is discouraged. Our tour packages include experienced mountain drivers with all-terrain vehicles — this matters more in winter than any other time.

Shoulder Season (September to October) — The Underrated Choice

🍂 Shoulder Season 5°C – 22°C

This is honestly our recommendation for most families and first-time visitors. After the monsoon clears, Himachal turns golden and auburn. The air is washed clean, the skies are startlingly blue, and the mountains are sharply visible in a way they're not always in summer haze. Crowds have dropped, but Rohtang is still usually open through September.

  • Golden autumn colours — Chinar and Poplar trees in full autumn glory
  • Clear mountain views — some of the best photography conditions of the year
  • Moderate temperatures — no extreme heat or cold
  • Rohtang usually open through September, sometimes early October
  • 15–20% lower hotel rates than peak summer
  • Apple harvest season — Himachali apple markets are a unique experience
Best for: Families, senior travellers, couples, photography trips

Which Season for Which Traveller?

For families with school-going children: Summer is the only practical option due to school holidays. Go in April or early June to beat the peak rush. Book everything at least 6 weeks in advance.

For honeymoon couples: Winter (December–January) wins hands down. Snow-covered Solang Valley, cosy fireplace hotels, fewer crowds, and a romantic atmosphere that peak summer simply can't match. Hotels often run honeymoon packages with better amenities in the off-season.

For adventure seekers: Summer for rafting and Rohtang; winter for skiing and snowboarding. Adventure activities are genuinely different across seasons, so it depends on what you want to do.

For senior travellers: September–October is the safest choice — mild temperatures, good roads, and a pace that allows leisurely sightseeing without fighting crowds. Avoid December–February due to road conditions and extreme cold, which can be hard on older travellers.

For photography: September–October for landscape and golden-hour shots; December–January for dramatic snow and mist compositions; April–May for wildflowers and waterfalls.

One Thing Most Guides Won't Tell You

Avoid the last two weeks of June and the entire month of July if you can. This is monsoon period — roads to Rohtang frequently close due to landslides, river activities are suspended when the Beas floods, and rainfall can be persistent. Hotels are cheap but the experience is genuinely limited. If this is your only travel window, call us and we'll work around it — but it's not our first recommendation.
