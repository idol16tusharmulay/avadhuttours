Overview

Why Kerala?

Kerala is a destination that simply cannot be rushed. "God's Own Country" lives up to its name with a sensory richness that is hard to match anywhere in India — the lazy drift of a traditional rice-boat houseboat through Alleppey's palm-fringed backwaters at dusk, the misty green stillness of Munnar's tea estates at dawn, the pungent sweetness of cardamom and cinnamon from a Thekkady spice garden in full bloom.

For cultural travellers, Fort Kochi is a museum without walls — Chinese fishing nets that have stood for 600 years, a Portuguese-built church, Dutch palaces, and Jewish synagogues all within a 2 km walk. Kathakali performances, Ayurvedic spas, and the unique Kerala Sadya feast banana-leaf banquet make this the most culturally immersive destination in peninsular India.

Families appreciate Kerala's safety, cleanliness and child-friendly wildlife experiences at Periyar Tiger Reserve in Thekkady. Honeymooners choose it for the romance of private houseboats. Must-See Places

Top Attractions in Kerala

🛶

Alleppey Houseboat Stay

Drift through 900 km of backwater canals on a Kerala kettuvallam. Overnight stays include onboard chef serving fresh catch and traditional Kerala meals.

🌿

Munnar Tea Gardens

Emerald-green tea estates stretch across rolling hills at 1,600 m. Visit the Tea Museum, Eravikulam National Park and Top Station viewpoint for cloud-level panoramas.

🐘

Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary

Boat safaris on Periyar Lake offer sightings of wild elephants, gaur and sambar deer. Thekkady also has guided jungle treks and spice plantation tours.

⛵

Fort Kochi Chinese Fishing Nets

The iconic cantilevered fishing nets of Fort Kochi are a photographer's dream at sunset. Buy the fresh catch from fishermen and have it cooked at a nearby stall.

🏖️

Kovalam Beach

Three crescent beaches near Thiruvananthapuram — Lighthouse Beach, Hawah Beach and Samudra Beach. Excellent for swimming, parasailing and beachside Ayurvedic massage.

🌶️

Thekkady Spice Plantation

Walk through spice estates growing pepper, cardamom, cloves, cinnamon and vanilla. Buy directly from farmers — quality and price far better than any city shop.

Insider Advice

Travel Tips for Kerala

  1. Book the houseboat two months ahead: Premium AC houseboats on the Vembanad and Ashtamudi Lakes get booked solid from October to January. Confirm the exact boat (photos & ATDC registration) before paying any advance.
  2. Fly into Kochi for North Kerala, Trivandrum for South: Kerala is a long state — choose your entry airport based on your itinerary to avoid backtracking. Kochi works best for Munnar + Alleppey; Trivandrum for Kovalam + Thekkady.
  3. Try an authentic Kerala Sadya: A traditional Sadya meal on a banana leaf with 20+ dishes is served in many restaurants on Sundays and festivals. Ask your driver or hotel for the best local restaurant — not a hotel buffet.
  4. Carry a rain jacket even in winter: Munnar and Thekkady at 900–1,600 m can have sudden showers even in December–January. A compact rain jacket takes very little space and saves the day.
  5. Buy spices at the plantation, not the airport: Cardamom, black pepper and vanilla from Thekkady farms are 40–60% cheaper than at Kochi or Trivandrum airports — and fresher. Most plantations allow direct purchase after a tour.

Getting there

How to Reach

✈️

By Air

Three international airports: Kochi (COK), Trivandrum (TRV), Calicut (CCJ). Direct flights from every Indian metro.

🚆

By Train

Ernakulam Junction / Trivandrum Central are well-connected. Konkan Railway is the scenic Mumbai–Goa–Kerala route.

🚌

By Road

Overnight Volvo from Bengaluru (10h) or Chennai (12h). NH-66 along the coast is well-maintained.

⛵

Houseboat Pickup

Most Alleppey houseboats pick up directly from the jetty — 1.5h drive from Kochi airport.

Eat like a local

Coastal & Spice Cuisine

Food is half the trip. Here's what to seek out — every Avadhut itinerary leaves room for tasting the local specialties.

  • Sadhya — vegetarian feast of 20+ items on a banana leaf
  • Karimeen Pollichathu — pearl-spot fish grilled in banana leaf
  • Meen Curry — coconut-based fish curry with kokum
  • Appam + Stew — lacy rice pancake with coconut-milk chicken stew
  • Puttu & Kadala — steamed rice cylinders with black chickpea curry
  • Beef Fry — Kerala-Christian spiced dry beef
  • Banana Chips — coconut-oil fried, the Kerala staple snack
  • Fresh Tender Coconut — ₹40 anywhere on the highway
