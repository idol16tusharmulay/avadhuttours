Overview

Why Himachal Pradesh?

Himachal Pradesh is northern India's most versatile hill destination — a single state that gives you the colonial charm of Shimla, the snow-carpeted slopes of Manali, the roaring Beas River at Kullu, and the spiritual serenity of ancient deodar forests. For Mumbai families escaping the summer heat, Himachal is the go-to escape — cool, safe, and accessible by road or air.

Manali, at 2,050 m, is the starting point for most Himalayan adventures. From here you can drive up to Solang Valley for zorbing and snow activities, push further to the legendary Rohtang Pass (3,978 m) for panoramic Himalayan views, or simply sit by the Beas and breathe clean mountain air. The Hadimba Devi Temple, set in a cedar grove, is one of the most atmospheric temples in India.

Shimla, the former summer capital of British India, offers a completely different flavour — Victorian architecture, the famous Mall Road for evening strolls, the Toy Train railway, and Kufri for short hikes and pony rides. Must-See Places

Top Attractions in Himachal Pradesh

🛍️

Shimla Mall Road

The social heart of Shimla — a pedestrian-only promenade lined with colonial-era shops, cafés and the historic Gaiety Theatre. Perfect for an evening stroll.

❄️

Solang Valley Snow Point

Just 14 km from Manali, Solang Valley offers zorbing, skiing, ropeway rides and picturesque snow fields — a perennial family favourite even in summer.

🏔️

Rohtang Pass

At 3,978 m, Rohtang separates the Kullu Valley from Lahaul & Spiti. Snow persists here year-round — an unforgettable drive through clouds and glaciers.

🌊

Kullu River Rafting

The Beas River between Kullu and Manali offers Grade III–IV rapids, perfect for beginners and thrill-seekers alike. Best from May to July.

🛕

Hadimba Devi Temple

A 500-year-old pagoda-style temple set in a dense cedar forest in Old Manali. A sacred, serene spot that feels far removed from the tourist bustle.

🐴

Kufri Day Trip

15 km from Shimla, Kufri offers pony rides, the Himalayan Wildlife Zoo and panoramic views. Easy half-day trip — ideal for families with young children.

Insider Advice

Travel Tips for Himachal Pradesh

  1. Get Rohtang Pass permits in advance: Only 1,200 vehicles are allowed per day via the National Green Tribunal order. Apply online at rohtangpermits.nic.in at least 2 days ahead.
  2. Book snow gear in Manali — don't carry it: Warm overcoats, boots and gloves for the snow can be rented at Solang Valley for ₹150–300 per set. No need to pack bulky gear from Mumbai.
  3. Travel by Volvo bus or fly to Bhuntar: Night Volvo buses from Delhi to Manali take 13–14 hours and are comfortable. Alternatively, fly to Bhuntar (Kullu Airport) — just 50 km from Manali.
  4. Avoid July–August if road safety is a concern: Monsoon brings landslides on NH3 (Manali-Rohtang stretch). Check HRTC road updates before travel during this period.
  5. Try local dhabas in Old Manali: The best Himachali food — Sidu (steamed wheat bread with ghee), Dham (festive thali) and fresh Beas river trout — is served in small local eateries near Hadimba Temple, not hotel restaurants.
