Overview

Why Rajasthan?

Rajasthan is India's most theatrical destination — a place where history is not behind glass in a museum but still very much alive in towering sandstone forts, ornate havelis painted in brilliant indigo, and markets ablaze with handblock-printed textiles, silver jewellery and hand-hammered copperware. The Pink City of Jaipur, Blue City of Jodhpur and Golden City of Jaisalmer each have a distinct personality that rewards slow exploration.

The Thar Desert, covering 60% of the state, is not a barren wasteland but a living landscape of shifting golden dunes, hardy desert villages, and astonishing sunset skies. A camel safari at Sam Sand Dunes followed by a night in a tented desert camp under the stars — with folk musicians and Rajasthani dancers around a bonfire — is an experience that stays with travellers for decades.

Rajasthan is also India's most accessible heritage tourism destination, with excellent road connectivity and a strong hospitality infrastructure ranging from budget guesthouses to converted royal palaces. Must-See Places

Top Attractions in Rajasthan

🏰

Amber Fort, Jaipur

The grand hilltop Amber Fort is Jaipur's crown jewel — intricate mirror work (Sheesh Mahal), elephant rides up the cobblestone ramp, and stunning views of Maota Lake below.

🏯

Mehrangarh Fort, Jodhpur

One of India's largest forts, rising 120 m above the Blue City. The seven-gated fortress houses an outstanding museum of royal weapons, palanquins and miniature paintings.

✨

Jaisalmer Golden Fort

The Sonar Quila (Golden Fort) is a living fort — 3,000 people still reside inside. Golden sandstone walls glow in the late afternoon sun, creating an otherworldly sight.

🐪

Sam Sand Dunes Camel Safari

45 km from Jaisalmer, the Sam dunes rise to 30 m. Camel safaris at sunset, followed by overnight stays in luxury tented camps with folk performances under the stars.

🌬️

Hawa Mahal, Jaipur

The "Palace of Winds" with its 953 latticed windows was built for royal women to observe street festivities unseen. The pink sandstone facade is Jaipur's most-photographed landmark.

🌙

Desert Camp Night

A night in the Thar Desert under a canopy of stars is transformative. Premium camps near Sam include bonfire dinners, Rajasthani folk music, puppet shows and stargazing sessions.

Insider Advice

Travel Tips for Rajasthan

  1. Hire a local guide at forts: ASI guides at Amber Fort, Mehrangarh and Jaisalmer Fort bring history alive with stories that no audio guide can match. Rates are fixed — agree before starting.
  2. Pack a warm layer for desert nights: Daytime in November–February can be 20–25°C, but desert nights in Jaisalmer drop to 5–8°C. A fleece jacket is non-negotiable for the desert camp experience.
  3. Drink only bottled water: Rajasthan's water is high in total dissolved solids. Carry a 1-litre bottle and replenish regularly — dehydration is common, especially in shoulder months like March and October.
  4. Buy textiles in Jodhpur, not Jaipur: Jodhpur's Sardar Market and Ghanta Ghar area have better prices and quality for block-printed fabric, Jodhpuri suits and leather goods than tourist-facing Jaipur markets.
  5. Book Jaisalmer Desert Camps in advance: Premium camps at Sam Dunes book out 2–3 months ahead from November to January. Basic camps are available walk-in, but the experience difference between premium and basic is significant.

Getting there

How to Reach

✈️

By Air

Jaipur (JAI) is the main hub. Direct flights also to Udaipur (UDR), Jodhpur (JDH) and Jaisalmer (JSA — limited).

🚆

By Train

Delhi–Jaipur Shatabdi (4.5h) is fast and comfortable. Mandor / Marudhar Express to other cities. Palace on Wheels for luxury circuit.

🚌

By Road

NH-48 Delhi → Jaipur (5h on expressway). Inter-city distances are large — break into 2–3 cities per trip.

🏰

Heritage Trains

Maharajas' Express and Royal Rajasthan on Wheels — week-long luxury train circuits.

Eat like a local

Royal Rajputana Cuisine

Food is half the trip. Here's what to seek out — every Avadhut itinerary leaves room for tasting the local specialties.

  • Dal-Baati-Churma — baked wheat balls with lentils & sweet crumble
  • Laal Maas — fiery red mutton curry with Mathania chillies
  • Gatte ki Sabzi — gram-flour dumplings in yogurt gravy
  • Ker Sangri — desert berry & bean stir-fry (Marwari)
  • Pyaaz Kachori — Jodhpur's onion-stuffed deep-fried snack
  • Mawa Kachori — Jaipur's sweet milk-stuffed pastry
  • Ghewar — disc-shaped honey-soaked monsoon sweet
  • Bajre ki Khichdi — millet & lentil winter porridge
