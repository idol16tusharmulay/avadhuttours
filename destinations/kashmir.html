Overview

Why Kashmir?

Kashmir, often called "Paradise on Earth," is a land of breathtaking natural beauty — a valley cradled between the mighty Himalayas and the Pir Panjal range. The iconic Dal Lake with its floating gardens and hand-carved wooden shikaras, the snow-blanketed meadows of Gulmarg, and the pine-forested valleys of Pahalgam combine to create a destination unlike any other in India.

Beyond the landscape, Kashmir enchants with its rich culture — intricate Pashmina shawls woven by skilled craftsmen, saffron farms that colour the fields purple every autumn, and the warmth of a Kashmiri household offering a cup of fragrant Kahwa. The Mughal Gardens of Srinagar, laid out by emperors centuries ago, remain among the finest formal gardens on the subcontinent.

Whether you are a couple seeking a romantic shikara ride at sunset, a family hoping to see their first snowfall, or an adventure seeker eyeing Gulmarg's gondola and ski slopes, Kashmir delivers memories that last a lifetime. Must-See Places

Top Attractions in Kashmir

🚣

Dal Lake Shikara Ride

Glide across the iconic Dal Lake in a hand-carved wooden shikara at sunrise or sunset. Visit the floating vegetable markets and Nehru Park island.

🚡

Gulmarg Gondola

Asia's highest cable car lifts you to 4,200 m — spectacular views of Nanga Parbat and untouched Himalayan snowfields. Ski resort in winter.

🌿

Pahalgam Meadows

The "Valley of Shepherds" offers Baisaran meadows, Aru Valley and the Lidder River — perfect for pony rides and family picnics in pine forests.

🏞️

Betaab Valley

Named after a Bollywood film shot here, Betaab Valley near Pahalgam stuns with its crystal-clear stream, pine forest backdrop and lush green carpet.

🌸

Mughal Gardens

Nishat Bagh, Shalimar Bagh and Chashme Shahi — three terraced Mughal gardens above Dal Lake are ablaze with tulips in April and May.

🏔️

Sonamarg Glacier

The "Meadow of Gold" sits at 2,740 m en route to Ladakh. Thajiwas Glacier is a short pony ride away — a must for first-time snow lovers.

Insider Advice

Travel Tips for Kashmir

  1. Book houseboats early for May–June: Dal Lake houseboats fill up fast during peak season. Confirm the exact houseboat you'll stay in before paying — photos can be misleading.
  2. Carry cash: Many remote areas including Gulmarg and Pahalgam markets have limited ATMs and spotty card connectivity. Keep ₹5,000–10,000 in cash.
  3. Layer your clothing: Even in June, evenings in Gulmarg drop to 8–10°C. Pack a fleece, a windproof jacket, and comfortable walking shoes.
  4. Pre-book the Gulmarg Gondola online: The gondola sells out by 10 AM in peak season. Book tickets at skiindia.com/gondola the evening before to avoid long queues.
  5. Respect local customs: Kashmir is predominantly Muslim — dress modestly near mosques and markets, avoid alcohol in public, and always ask before photographing locals.

Getting there

How to Reach

✈️

By Air

Direct flights to Srinagar (SXR) from Mumbai (3h), Delhi (1.5h), Bengaluru and Pune. Most cost-effective in Apr–May and Sep–Oct.

🚆

By Train

Trains run only as far as Jammu Tawi; from there a 7–9h scenic drive on NH-44 over the Banihal Pass.

🚌

By Road

Volvo / SUV from Jammu, Pathankot, or Delhi (via Udhampur). Picturesque but 12+ hours from Delhi — best broken into two days.

🚁

Helicopter

Srinagar–Vaishno Devi helicopter for combined pilgrimage + Kashmir tours.

Eat like a local

Kashmiri Wazwan

Food is half the trip. Here's what to seek out — every Avadhut itinerary leaves room for tasting the local specialties.

  • Rogan Josh — slow-cooked lamb in red Kashmiri chilli & yogurt
  • Yakhni — light yogurt-based mutton curry
  • Dum Aloo — baby potatoes in spiced gravy
  • Modur Pulao — sweet saffron rice with dry fruits
  • Kahwa — green tea with saffron, almonds & cinnamon
  • Tabak Maaz — twice-cooked lamb ribs
  • Trout — fresh from Lidder & Sindh rivers
  • Wazwan feast — 7–36 course wedding banquet (advance order)
